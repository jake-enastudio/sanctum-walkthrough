Risk Factors

Risks & Mitigants

Key risks identified with corresponding mitigation strategies.

Construction Overrun
Medium Impact
5% contingency buffer built into budget. Phased construction allows early revenue from gym/recovery while later phases complete. Fixed-price contractor agreements where possible.
Membership Ramp
Medium Impact
Conservative base case assumes 250 members by Year 2 in a market with 15,000+ expats within 15 minutes. Pre-launch membership campaign to build waitlist before opening.
Regulatory / Permitting
High Impact
Experienced local legal counsel engaged. PMA structure established with proper foreign ownership compliance. No construction begins until permits secured.
FX Exposure (IDR/USD)
Low Impact
Membership and villa pricing in USD. F&B tenant leases denominated in USD. Primary cost exposure is local labor and materials, which benefit from IDR depreciation.
Competition
Low Impact
No direct competitor in Bali combines gym, thermal recovery, F&B, and villas on a riverfront site. The natural setting creates a structural moat that flat-land competitors cannot replicate.
Legal Structure

Entity & Governance

Investment Vehicle
PT PMA (foreign-owned Indonesian limited liability company) established in compliance with Indonesian investment law. All investor equity held through the PT.
Investor Protections
Board observer rights, quarterly financial reporting, annual audited statements, anti-dilution provisions, and pre-emptive rights on future rounds.
Reporting Cadence
Monthly operational dashboard, quarterly financial statements with management commentary, annual audited financials by independent auditor.
Governing Law
Indonesian law governs the PT PMA. Shareholder agreement includes Singapore-seated arbitration clause for dispute resolution.
